As part of Fresh Tilled Soil's AUX bootcamp, I completed a redesign of Spirit Airline's checkout flow. This process consisted of user interviews, wireframing, and visual design.

User Research

To begin, I interviewed someone in Spirit's demographic - people looking for cheap, no-frills plane tickets. I was attempting to understand what is important to a user searching for this type of flight. The following quotes were some of the stand-outs from the interview:

“I usually think I can put together something cheaper than the site, but I never can.”

“I would like some helpful information about the airports.”

“There’s no way it costs this much.”

“I don’t trust airlines to give me a good deal.”

“My biggest anxiety with airports & flying is how I get to it and how it get from it to where I'm going"

“Usually I’m excited, but the less I want to go and the more expensive it is, the more stressed I am.”

Insights

I distilled this interview into some driving insights:

Price is key. People go to Spirit airlines for the low cost.

People feel like airlines are charging more than necessary, or arbitrarily.

Very few users looking for a low price will purchase on their first visit to a website.

The emotional state of the user is deeply tied to the nature of their trip.

A lot of the stress around purchasing plane tickets is caused by the obfuscated pricing scheme. Because humans are loss averse, they do not like to feel as if they are being cheated out of a good price. Because of this, I wanted to make sure the user felt they were getting a good deal. These are some of the main ideas I had for the redesign:

Prioritize price when returning search results.

Remember searches of returning users and make it easy to continue a search.

Provide text / email updates to users about changing prices for a trip.

Tailor the purchasing / flying experience based on user profile (business, travel, personal).

Highlight high-value flights (combination of price and length).
