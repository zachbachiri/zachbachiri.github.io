'Are We There Yet?' is a project that I lead for Scout, Northeastern's student-run design studio. It was a design thinking project that was worked on with two professors at Northeastern. We were tasked with finding a way to reduce carbon emissions through education. We went through the full design thinking process, doing broad background research, talking with academic experts, getting continuous feedback from relevant stakeholders, conducting field research before forming opinions and ideating. We decided to focus on graduating college students, as new transportation behaviors will be formed when they presumably are getting new jobs and forming routines. We designed an experiment to test our theory of positive reinforcement as motivation.

Our experiment consists of an experimental group and a control group- both college age students. To begin the experiment, both groups would run through a scenario that we designed, shown on the left. The scenario simulates what it's like to move to a new city after graduating college. Participants will make transportation decisions that will help us to calculate how “green” they might be in real life. Then for the next three months, the experimental group will receive positive reinforcement about their real life behavior. The control group will receive no positive reinforcement. After 3 months, both groups will take the same simulation and we will calculate if the experimental group makes greener choices.
