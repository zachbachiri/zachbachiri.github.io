Background

'Are We There Yet?' is a project that I lead for Scout, Northeastern's student-led design studio. This design thinking project was worked on with two professors at Northeastern. We were tasked with finding a way to reduce carbon emissions through education. This meant going through the full design thinking process: doing broad background research, talking with academic experts, getting continuous feedback from relevant stakeholders, and conducting field research before forming opinions and ideating. We decided to focus on graduating college students because we know that new transportation behaviors will be formed when people are presumably getting new jobs and forming new transportation routines. Our research led us to positive reinforcement through education as a valuable solution. Whereas many previous approaches had focused on informing people of the negatives effects of their behavior, we believed encouraging existing eco-friendly behavior is more valuable. We designed an experiment to test this theory.

Our experiment consisted of an experimental group and a control group, both college age students, that would run through the scenario shown above. The scenario simulates what it's like to move to a new city after graduating college(getting a job, picking an apartment, finding a grocery store, etc). Participants make transportation decisions that will help us to calculate how “green” they might be in real life. Then, for the next three months, the experimental group will receive positive reinforcement about their real life behavior via email. The control group will receive no positive reinforcement. After 3 months, both groups will complete the same simulation and we will calculate any change in behavior. After we created this experiment, we handed the project off to the professors to be developed and administered, which is currently in progress.
