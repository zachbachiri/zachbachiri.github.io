Spot is a mobile application that allows co-located users to create a collective music playlist for their party. Users can all dynamically add songs to a playlist and vote for their favorite songs. This project was completed for the Human-Computer Interaction course at Northeastern University with two other students. We created personas, storyboards, and wireframes; ran usability tests with paper prototypes; created high fidelity mockups; and prototyped the appliction with HTML and CSS. I contributed heavily to all aspects of the design process and was fully responsible for the visual design.

high fidelity mockups

The Problem

At social gatherings, many people wish to play a role in selecting the music. Though, there is no good way for multiple people to create a playlist that pleases everybody. Spot attempts to solve this problem by creating a system where everyone at a party can voice their opinion and contribute songs to a playlist.

Sketching and Flow

Through sketching and storyboarding, we defined the flow of the app.

Testing

We conducted multiple iterations of usability testing to identify and solve various usability issues. Testing was completed with paper prototypes and, later, an html prototype. These tests specifically helped in defining the "Join a party" and "Add A Song" interactions.
