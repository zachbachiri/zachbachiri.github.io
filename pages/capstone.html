Background

For my senior capstone project at Northeastern University, I constructed a digital interactive art project with 1 classmate. Our goal for this project was to create an interaction that encourages people to experience a familiar space in a new and interesting way. We had designed an installation that would consist of 15 interactive pillars spread out over a 400 sq ft space. As people enter the space, pillars will light up at varying intensity based on someone's proximity to the pillar. On top of each pillar there will be a button that produces a burst of sound and light unique to each pillar. And based on the amount movement in the space, music will be playing and increasing in intensity in relation to the movement.

Pivot

Due to time and financial constraints, we were forced to reconsider our design and reduce our scope. We then designed an installation in the stairwell of our Art + Design building. We installed sensors on each stair and mounted LEDs on the wall next to the steps that reacted to people stepping on the stairs. We constructed this installation using an Arduino, LEDs, and custom force-sensitive resistors. We handled all of the wiring and soldering ourselves.
