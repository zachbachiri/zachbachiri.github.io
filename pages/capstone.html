exploring physical interaction
testing our proximity lighting code

For my senior capstone project at Northeastern University, I am working on a digital interactive art project with 1 classmate. Our goal for this project is to create an interaction that encourages people to experience a familiar space in a new and interesting way. We have begun to design an installation that would consist of 15 interactive pillars spread out over a 400 sq ft space. As people enter the space, pillars will light up at varying intensity based on someone's proximity to the pillar. On top of each pillar there will be a button that produces a burst of sound and light unique to each pillar.

Currently, we are prototyping individuals pillars as we design the interactions. We are using Arduinos, LED strips, ultrasonic rangefinder sensors for the electronics of the pillar, and have used various materials to prototype the structure of the pillar. We started out with cardboard and paper, and our most recent prototype uses a wood frame and rigid plastic covering.
